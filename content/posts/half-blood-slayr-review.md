## The Sound

This album is a topic I could go on about for ages. I have no clue how I discovered it, but I've been listening to it regularly, and I've had the full album on loop since probably November 2025, and I actually believe it has degraded over time, and it's not at all me growing out of it, and I can explain now.

At first, it was released, and it was perfect, then it got taken down.

Then it came back, but missing a couple of tracks such as 24/7 and Power 4. This ruined the transitions of the tracks, then it got reworked to work without them, which still didn't make the transitions hit right. etc, on the final version that we have now, we are missing power 4, even though it has been released as a single, which is disappointing because the transition between the tracks around it still doesn't go as perfectly as it did into the original track.

This album I disliked at first, believe it or not, I thought it was sloppy rage rap with mumbly lyrics, but listening to it more and more, I got even more and more stuff stuck in my head, ad and I started to appreciate the amazing production from Waera and slayr, which has such a refined melodic sound and so many iconic leads and whatnot. At this stage, I would absolutely go to a show of his to hear this album as I think it's damn near perfect for a melodic, digi-rage rap album.

I chose the standard version of the album as I believe the deluxe BloodLuxe is actually a downgrade from the original. I'm unsure why I think this, but it doesn't have the same vibe as the original, well-polished first release of halfblood.

Anyways, the album contains many bouncy beats which all provide a different vibe and tone with incredible transitions between each one of them, and it shows slayrs versatility as an artist. Most of this album is produced by Waera mainly but I've seen slayr be very involved with it, waera has been on a roll lately and dare I say he may be currently going further than slayr himself as he lately produced for many people, including ericdoa.

## Verdict

Great album, which I don't understand the hate around, of course, you have to dabble in the genre to enjoy it. Great release from slayr and a nice diverse improvement from his previous work but as shown in the deluxe and some singles since, it seems he's diverting to a bit more of a strict ragey sound which I don't think I prefer.
