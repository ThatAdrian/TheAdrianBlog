## The Sound

Long-awaited release for me. Keep in mind this is a day one review and this may change as time passes. I've heard 3 of the songs when I saw Glaive and Kurtains live in London, and they spoke about a collab album coming out soon and I've been stoked since. The songs I heard live include, the troubles, the band and Let's Link Up and Die, and the experience was phenomenal.

I think Glaive and Kurtains complement each other so well in the music they create and they showcase that off very well. At first I thought this would be a hype filled album, which is sort of what I was hoping for mainly due to Kurtains saying in the track the troubles, "not one sad song on the record", but I was surprised to hear so many acoustic instrumental parts rather than mainly electronic and digicore songs like the troubles and the band.

By all means, I am not disappointed in this difference as they both perform very well in either side of their genres and all of these tracks are fantastic.

This album starts off with Weston Super Mare, this song includes a lovely guitar instrumental with simple drums and a nice compilation of vocals from Kurtains and Glaive simultaneously, it's a great pop-like track. Next is Half Two, which is another guitar instrumental track with them both singing, this one sounds a little more generic than the other one, but it's quite good nonetheless. I only say this because I'm struggling to understand what they're really saying. Afterwards is what I believe to be one of the standout tracks of this album, the band, amazing hype upbeat digicore instrumental with a 4 to the floor kick going along and heavy sidechaining, they have an amazing flow on this song and I will be having this song on loop for the foreseeable future. Afterwards is yaya toure, which is a good track, nice pads in the back, no clue what theyre really saying here again but its nice and catchy, more of a somber track and then it goes into this beautiful acoustic track, spinning around and this song is such a beautiful medley starting with a nice piano, then some gentle acoustic guitar with glaive and kurtains doing some slow singing, this track is gentle and very pretty, i like it. Afterwards is the interlude where they embrace their inner wild west cowboy, general campfire song, strange but ok. After that is the front, mainly sung by Kurtains, and this song is great, he has a great flow and it's super catchy, I'm mildly annoyed it's only 1 minute and 40 seconds, overall a good song and I think I heard this snippet a while back and it was stuck in my head for a while. After that short song it goes onto lets link up and die which is decent, it was a lot better hearing it live cause everyone was jumping and a lot of people noticed he samples yoshi city by yung lean I believe, I think its decent but it doesnt translate the hype from hearing it live to hearing it now, nevertheless it's good and it's going on my playlist, I can see this song getting stuck in my head for a while. Next is a split track unca / composure, i think unca is a bit generic but its not bad nothing revolutionary, id give that one a 3.5 but composure is quite good, I'll give that one a 4, I chose to round up on the general track cause unca doesnt drag it down as a song, composure raises it, kurtains does great on this song, this boy has some pipes on him. It's catchy and smooth. Onwards to the next track, flyen, nothing crazy but a genuine pop rock kinda track, not too shabby. Next is the troubles, this song came out as a single prior to the albums release, I think its great, they both do their part and it's super hype, I remember jumping to this track when I saw them live, only thing I dislike about it is the way glaives first verse starts, something about that ticks me off but after that its super good, great eletronic track with a strong saw wave from the start, already has been on loop and will continue to be. Finally, we have crisis, with strong guitar and vocals, also more somber but it's a good song yet again and a strong finish to the album from both of them.

I think both glaive and kurtains deserve more exposure, they're possibly the best duo out now and incredible individually. They're doing all of it independently with no label.

## Standout Tracks

The band, Spinning around, the front and the troubles

## Verdict

Worthy listen and a good album from Glaive and Kurtains, it's not better than Y'all by Glaive, but that's okay. Glaive mentioned they may be going on tour. I hope they come to the UK as I will be going if I'm able to. Solid release, and I look forward to seeing what comes from it.
