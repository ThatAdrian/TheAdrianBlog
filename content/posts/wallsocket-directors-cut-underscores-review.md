## The Sound

This album is incredible. An actual masterpiece the emotions and story it portrays throughout the tracks is insane and the world building about the little town in michigan or whatever it is is great. Both the acoustic and the production of the tracks are all impeccable and i don't think there is a single skip throughout this album, even the songs that didn't go onto my playlist all have full listen value to them where it enhances the full listening of the album and the differences in characters and moods in the story. 

The record starts off with 2 insane upbeat tracks with "cops and robbers" and "locals with gabby start", super jumpy, super hype, very summery and enjoyable and then it transitions into 2 incredibly deep, eerie and actually scary songs. Each time i listen through i get goosebumps on multiple tracks especially "duhhhh" and "you don't even know who i am". then it goes back to super upbeat anthem "johnny johnny johnny" which is an incredibly catchy and enjoyable song although it is about quite traumatising and touchy topics. April (underscores) mentions in a recent song "Point A" that her biggest regret is ever making that song which is shocking since it's such a good song. Then it goes onto some even creepier tracks with "shoot to kill, kill your darlings" and "horror movie soundtrack" which for a very long time i thought were one track but no they just meld into each other incredibly well. After that it transitions back into a super upbeat song "old money bitch", again a super catchy summery song to sing along to in the car, going onto a hectic song with henhouse! called "geez louise" which is a good song but less replay value in my opinion, still a great track though. Moving onto "seventyseven dog years" which is more acoustic and rock like transferring into "uncanny long arms with jane remover" which is amazing as a rock song, very noise filled but the drop at 4:30 near the end is immaculate and i wish it was longer. Afterwards there's a mellow track called "good luck final girl" with deep closing story and information which really levels out and cools down the mood in a strange way which is a great way to close out the original album. 

Then as if that masterpiece wasn't enough we get a brilliant deluxe with 4 very nice tracks. I hate a deluxe which is worse than the original. There's one I'll speak about in a future review but this one starting off with "My guy" which is another catchy sing along song which may be the track that shows up most on my shuffle, moving onto "northwest zombie girl"
